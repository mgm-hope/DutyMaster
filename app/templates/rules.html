Current auto-assign algorithm

1. Hard rules first. For every duty, DutyMaster first keeps any pre-filled assignments, then removes anyone who is not allowed for that duty, is teaching at that time, is out of school, has a one-off exclusion, has reached a max duty or max lunch-duty limit, would break a custom hard rule, or would clash with another duty. Excluded staff are invisible to scoring.

2. Additional staff lunch rule. ESLT, Chaplaincy and Admin are only considered for lunch duties. They are never used for non-lunch duties. For lunch, they are used by priority: ESLT first, then Chaplaincy, then Admin, while respecting their own max duty limits.

3. Slot order. Lunch duties are processed first. All remaining duties are processed by the number of eligible teaching staff available: the slot with the fewest available teaching staff is filled first. Equal slots keep stable database order, with no randomisation.

4. Teacher score. Eligible teaching staff start at 100 points. DutyMaster adds 10 points for every free period still remaining across the two-week cycle. If the duty would use the teacher's last free period that day, 1 point is deducted. The score is recalculated fresh for every slot.

5. SLT balance. For duties that are SLT-only, DutyMaster applies the Balanced SLT Duty Distribution rule before the normal teacher score. It offers or assigns the eligible SLT member with the fewest duties so far, then uses heavy-duty count, recent non-free streak, and initials as stable tie-breakers.

6. Tie-breaker. If teaching staff have the same score, DutyMaster looks backwards from the duty slot and deducts 1 point for each consecutive non-free period since the teacher's most recent free period. The shortest busy streak wins. If still tied, initials provide a stable final order.

7. Output checks. The Proposed Timetable shows the rota, blank-duty reasons, workload by role, fairness notes, and lunch duties where additional staff were used.

How custom rules are applied

The allocator first applies the built-in DutyMaster rules, then applies active hard custom rules. If a member of staff fails any hard custom rule, they are removed from the available list for that duty. If every possible assignment would break a rule, the duty is left blank and recorded for manual review instead of forcing a weak allocation. The final fair ordering still uses available periods, duty count, heavy-duty count, and recent allocations.
